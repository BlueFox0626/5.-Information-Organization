Our mission: help everyone pursue their craft and improve their lives

Bloc is an online education company with intensive mentor-led programs for aspiring developers and designers.

Bloc relies on the time-tested apprenticeship model to help students start new careers. We combine experienced mentors and project-based curriculum to offer students around the world a more accessible and effective way alternative to traditional degrees or in-person bootcamps.

Leadership

Clint Schmidt
CEO
Biography
Clint Schmidt is a veteran of e-commerce and online marketplaces. Clint has held executive marketing, business development, and product management roles at Half.com, eBay, 1-800-CONTACTS, Livemocha, Roblox, Wantful, and Good Eggs, along with a stint at Harrison Metal, a leading seed-stage venture capital firm in Silicon Valley. Dave Paola
CTO and Cofounder
Biography
Dave Paola is a software engineer turned founder who learned to code at an early age. Prior to graduating with a computer science degree from the University of Illinois, he worked on hundreds of real projects at ACM, Brechbuhler Scales, the university, and an early-stage startup called Harmonize.fm. Dave co-founded Djangy.com, the world’s first python cloud hosting product, and subsequently joined Kontagent. Bloc is the result of Dave’s lifelong dissatisfaction with K-12 & higher education.
Riley End
VP of Growth and Operations
Biography
Riley End brings 8 years of operational, growth, analytics and business development experience to his role at Bloc. Riley began his career with Related, a real estate development and investment firm based in New York before moving into an operating role at Savored, an early-stage restaurant industry startup. In 2012, Riley became co-founder and CEO of Given Goods Co., a social impact ecommerce marketplace featuring over 2,000 products from 150 brands. Riley has an A.B. in Government from Dartmouth College.
Courtland Alves
VP of Product and Mentorship
Biography
Courtland Alves has 8 years of experience as a product manager, engineering manager and entrepreneur. He joins Bloc through an acquisition of DevBridge, which partnered with coding bootcamps to help their grads find jobs. Courtland has led product and engineering at Yahoo and CrowdStar and was CEO at Hitpost (acquired by Yahoo!). Courtland has a background in software engineering with a degree in Computer Science and Electrical Engineering from UC Berkeley.

Michael Dearing
Founder, Harrison Metal
Biography
While building Harrison Metal, Michael was a Consulting Associate Professor at Stanford University’s School of Engineering, teaching classes in Mechanical Engineering, Management Science & Engineering, and the d.school. After finishing his work at Stanford, he decided to continue teaching at Harrison Metal. Today Harrison Metal is a center for teaching and investing in exceptional people.
